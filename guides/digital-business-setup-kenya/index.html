Digital Business Setup in Kenya: The Systems Most Founders Skip

Many Kenyan founders launch with a logo, social page, and payment number—but without the operating system behind the business. That gap is why many digital businesses stall before their first stable month of sales.

1) Register and structure the business correctly

Start with your BN registration on eCitizen, then align your business name, payment details, and public-facing profiles so customers see one consistent identity.

2) Set up payment rails early

Decide how you will collect payments: M-Pesa, Paybill, and card rails like Paystack. Document how transactions are tracked and reconciled weekly.

3) Use professional business email

Use role-based addresses such as sales@, support@, and finance@. This avoids customer loss when one staff member leaves and keeps handovers clean.

4) Install lead tracking from day one

If leads are handled through WhatsApp or DMs only, revenue leaks fast. Use a lightweight CRM workflow and define follow-up stages.

5) Create task and delivery visibility

Use a simple task board to track owner, deadline, and status. This reduces delays and internal miscommunication.

6) Keep knowledge and SOPs in one place

Centralized SOPs help teams execute consistently, especially when teams are growing or distributed.
