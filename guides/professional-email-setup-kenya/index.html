2. Technical Setup: The "Big Three" DNS Records

To ensure your emails are delivered and not spoofed, you must add these records to your domain dashboard (e.g., Safaricom, Sasahost, or HostPinnacle).

MX Records (Mail Exchange)

This tells the world where to send your mail. If you use Zoho, you'll add mx.zoho.com. For Google, you'll use ASPMX.L.GOOGLE.COM.

SPF (Sender Policy Framework)

This is a TXT record that authorizes specific servers to send mail for your domain.
Google: v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all
Zoho: v=spf1 include:zoho.com ~all

DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)

DKIM acts as a digital signature. It ensures the email hasn't been modified in transit, proving it truly came from you.

3. Implementing DMARC

DMARC tells receiving servers what to do if SPF or DKIM fails. It is the ultimate defense against someone "spoofing" your email to scam your clients.

Starting Record: v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:admin@yourdomain.co.ke
Note: p=none allows you to monitor failures without blocking legitimate mail while you test your setup.

4. How to Stay Out of the Spam Folder

  • Warm-up: Don't send 1,000 emails on your first day. Start small.
  • Avoid Keywords: Stay away from "Free," "Cash," and "Win" in subject lines.
  • Unsubscribe: Always include a clear way for people to opt-out.
  • Blacklist Check: Regularly verify that your IP isn't flagged by spam filters.
