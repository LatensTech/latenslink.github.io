How to Write and Structure a Professional CV That Gets You Interviews

Category: Career Intel · CVs · Job Search · Kenya & Global

Most CVs don’t fail because the candidate is unqualified. They fail because the CV is poorly written, badly structured, visually outdated, or unreadable by ATS systems.

This career intel explains how to write, structure, and format a professional CV using modern tools like Canva — based on how recruiters and applicant tracking systems actually evaluate CVs in 2026.

What Makes a CV “Professional” Today?

  • Clear structure with logical section order
  • ATS-readable formatting (no broken columns or text boxes)
  • Achievement-focused bullet points, not task lists
  • Modern typography, spacing, and alignment
  • Easy to scan by recruiters in under 10 seconds

Why Structure and Writing Matter More Than Design

A professional CV is defined by how it is written and structured, not by visual effects. Tools like Canva work well only when used correctly — following recruiter-approved layouts and ATS-friendly formatting.

This walkthrough focuses on how to:

  • Write CV sections recruiters expect
  • Structure content without breaking ATS parsing
  • Format and export a CV correctly for job portals and email

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best CV format in Kenya?

A reverse-chronological, ATS-friendly CV with clean sections performs best across Kenyan and global employers.

Can I use Canva CV templates for job applications?

Yes, as long as the template is structured correctly and exported as a PDF.

How long should a professional CV be?

One page for early career professionals, two pages maximum for experienced roles.

Is writing a CV different from designing one?

Yes. Writing a CV focuses on structure, wording, and achievements, while design only affects presentation. Recruiters prioritize content clarity and ATS readability over visuals.

Can ATS systems read Canva CVs?

ATS systems can read Canva CVs if they are structured correctly, use simple layouts, and are exported properly as PDFs.

Should I include a photo on a Kenyan CV?

Photos are optional and role-dependent. For corporate, tech, and international roles, a photo is usually unnecessary.
